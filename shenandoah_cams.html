⚠️Black Bear & Trail SafetyFrequent sightings▾
🐻 Black bears are the park's top-billed safety topic — NPS's guidance is to stay at least 150 feet (about four bus-lengths) away from any bear, never approach for a photo, and store food properly. The second major theme is slips and falls on wet waterfall rocks — never walk on top of a waterfall. Deer, bear, and wild turkey regularly dart across Skyline Drive without warning, so watch your speed. Ticks are also an increasing concern — check yourself after every hike. NPS safety info ↗

Note No Timed Entry
Standard entry, no reservation
Unlike many western parks, Shenandoah has no park-wide vehicle reservation system — only Old Rag requires a separate day-use ticket (see below).

🎫Old Rag Day-Use Permit$2/person▾
🥾 Hiking Old Rag's Saddle, Ridge, or Ridge Access trails requires a day-use ticket, March 1 – November 30 — $2/person, separate from the park entrance fee. NPS releases 800 tickets/day: 400 thirty days out, 400 five days out, both via Recreation.gov. The main trailhead parking lot is closed May 4 – November 2026 for infrastructure work — overflow parking is available, and tickets are still required during the closure. NPS Old Rag FAQ ↗

Shenandoah does not hold a formal Dark Sky designation, given its proximity to the DC metro area — but on clear moonless nights NPS notes roughly 2,500 visible stars from spots like Big Meadows. The park has run an annual Night Sky Festival since 2016 with ranger-led programs. NPS night sky info ↗
