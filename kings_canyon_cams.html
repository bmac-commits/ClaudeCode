❄️Seasonal Access NoticeCedar Grove Rd closed▾
🚧 Cedar Grove Road (Hwy 180 into the canyon floor) closes every winter — typically mid-November through May, weather dependent. Only Grant Grove stays reachable year-round via Hwy 180. If you're planning a visit to Zumwalt Meadow, Roaring River Falls, or Road's End, check current road status before you go. NPS road conditions ↗
🪧Park Entrance & Fees$35 (shared w/ Sequoia)▾
Main Entrance Usually Open
Big Stump Entrance Station, Hwy 180
The only entrance station for Kings Canyon, reached via Hwy 180 from Fresno. Leads directly into Grant Grove Village.
💡 One fee covers both Kings Canyon & Sequoia National Parks — no separate payment needed if continuing on to Sequoia via Generals Highway. No live wait-time data is published for this station.
NPS directions ↗
To Sequoia Seasonal Conditions Vary
Generals Highway
Connects Grant Grove to the Giant Forest / Lodgepole area of Sequoia National Park — the two parks are administered jointly and share this connecting road.
💡 Chains or 4WD may be required in winter above ~5,000 ft. Check conditions before driving between the parks.
NPS directions ↗
Cedar Grove Closed in Winter
Hwy 180 Canyon Floor Road
The road from Grant Grove down into the canyon floor (Cedar Grove, Zumwalt Meadow, Road's End). Closes seasonally each year.
💡 Typically closed ~mid-November to ~May, weather dependent. Grant Grove remains accessible year-round even when this stretch is shut.

Kings Canyon is not an IDA-certified Dark Sky Park, but its high elevation and distance from Central Valley light pollution make for genuinely dark skies. The park co-hosts the annual Sequoia & Kings Canyon Dark Sky Festival with neighboring Sequoia National Park. Best viewing is on new-moon nights away from Grant Grove Village lighting — bring a red flashlight to preserve night vision. NPS night sky info ↗
