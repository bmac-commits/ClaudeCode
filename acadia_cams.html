⚠️Ocean & Rogue Wave SafetyThunder Hole risk▾
🌊 NPS is direct about this: "do not turn your back on the ocean" — rogue waves have killed visitors at Thunder Hole and swept people off oceanside ledges even in calm weather, most notably a 2009 incident that killed a 7-year-old near Thunder Hole. Rising tides can also cut off return routes along the shore. Stay well back from wet rocks and posted edges, and never climb over railings for a better view. Falls from granite cliffs are the park's other major hazard. NPS safety info ↗

🪧Park Entrance & Fees$35 · Cadillac Rd permit▾
🎫 Cadillac Summit Road requires a separate vehicle reservation, May 20 – Oct 25, 2026 — a $6/vehicle fee on top of the park entrance pass, booked via Recreation.gov. Choose a Sunrise ticket (a 90-minute entry window, roughly 4:00–7:30 AM depending on the month) or a Daytime ticket (30-minute windows, ~6 AM–8 PM). 30% of slots release 90 days out, 70% release 2 days out, both at 10 AM ET. Hulls Cove Visitor Center is the main gateway near Bar Harbor. Park Loop Road connects most of the island's key sights.
💡 The free Island Explorer shuttle (propane-powered, fare-free) runs seasonally and can save you from parking headaches in peak season.

🌊Bar Island Tide CrossingLive tide data▾
🚶 A gravel sandbar connects downtown Bar Harbor to Bar Island — but it's underwater except at low tide. Safe crossing window is roughly 1.5 hours before to 1.5 hours after low tide. Linger too long and you're stuck: NPS's own guidance is to hire a water taxi or wait up to 9 hours for the bar to reappear. NPS Bar Island info ↗
