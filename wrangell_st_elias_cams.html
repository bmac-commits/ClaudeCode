⚠️Remoteness & SafetySelf-rescue required▾
🧭 NPS's own words: "this is a truly vast and remote area without the usual safeguards one expects in a more developed National Park Service area." Opportunities for rescue and evacuation are slim and response time can be slow — visitors "must be prepared to self-rescue and to be self-sufficient." Cell service is largely absent, and NPS emphasizes preparation for extreme wilderness travel and weather-related delays. NPS safety info ↗

🚗McCarthy Road & KennecottFee-free park▾
🛣️ NPS quote: "Wrangell-St. Elias is a fee free park!" There is no entry gate on either park road — Nabesna Road (north) or McCarthy Road (center). The 59-mile gravel McCarthy Road runs from the Edgerton Highway near Chitina to the Kennicott River, where a pedestrian-only footbridge (no vehicle bridge) crosses to McCarthy — most rental car companies prohibit driving this road. Kennecott is ~5 miles beyond McCarthy, reached by shuttle, bike, or foot. NPS McCarthy Road info ↗

🏕CampingNo NPS campground at HQ▾
⛺ NPS is direct: "there are no places to camp inside Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve" near Copper Center — nearest options are BLM, Alaska State Parks, Chugach National Forest, or Ahtna Native Corporation lands (non-NPS). Along McCarthy Road/Kennecott, two primitive NPS sites do exist: Jumbo Creek Campground (1.4 mi north of Kennecott, hike-in only, bear boxes) and Donoho Basin Group Camping (3–6 mi north, groups of 8–12, registration required). Private campgrounds also operate along McCarthy Road. NPS camping info ↗
