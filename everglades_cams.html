🐊Wildlife & Hazard SafetyAlligators, heat, mosquitoes▾
🐊 NPS: never feed or approach alligators or crocodiles — keep at least 15 feet back, and never swim in freshwater areas after dusk. This is the only place on Earth where American alligators and American crocodiles live side by side. Mosquitoes are intense, especially in summer wet season (May–Nov) — repellent is essential. Heat index regularly exceeds 100°F in summer; carry more water than you think you need. Venomous snakes (cottonmouth, coral snake, rattlesnake) are present — watch your step off trails. 🎫Park Entrance & Fees3 entrances▾
South EntranceMain Entrance
Ernest F. Coe Visitor Center
Near Homestead, FL. Leads to Royal Palm (Anhinga Trail), Flamingo Visitor Center, and the main park road. Most-visited entrance.
Northeast EntranceShark Valley
Shark Valley Visitor Center
On US-41/Tamiami Trail. Separate entrance station, own fee — 15-mile paved loop road for biking, tram tours, and the observation tower.
🚤 Gulf Coast Entrance (Everglades City, NW corner) — gateway to the Ten Thousand Islands and Wilderness Waterway; primarily boat access.
