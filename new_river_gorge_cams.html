⚠️Water, Climbing & Gorge SafetyWhitewater, cliffs▾
🚣 The New River ranges from calm pools to serious Class III–V whitewater — never enter without appropriate experience, gear, and knowledge of current flows. Rock climbing here is world-class but genuinely dangerous without proper training and equipment; use the park's dedicated climbing safety guidance. Park roads are winding and narrow — drive carefully. Copperheads, ticks, and poison ivy are present throughout the gorge, and lightning and sudden temperature swings can catch hikers off guard.
