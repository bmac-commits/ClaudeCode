🪶A Sacred Site — Please Visit RespectfullyJune closure▾
🏔️ Devils Tower — known as Mato Tipila ("Bear Lodge") and by other names to more than 20 Northern Plains tribes — is a sacred site central to Lakota, Cheyenne, Kiowa, Crow and other tribal origin stories and ceremonies. Out of respect, please stay on trails near the base, keep prayer bundles/cloths tied to trees undisturbed, and be mindful during ceremonies. NPS history & culture ↗
🧗 Voluntary June Climbing Closure: Since the 1995 Climbing Management Plan, climbers are asked to voluntarily avoid climbing the Tower throughout June, when many tribal ceremonies take place. Compliance has historically been strong (90%+ reduction in June climbing), and the Access Fund and major guiding services publicly support the closure. Over 100 other climbing areas exist within a day's drive. NPS climbing info ↗
🪧Park Entrance & Fees1 open · $25▾
Only Entrance Usually Open
WY-110, off US-14 near Devils Tower Junction
Devils Tower has a single paved entrance road crossing the Belle Fourche River. Most visitors arrive via I-90 and US-14 from Sundance or Moorcroft, WY.
💡 Entrance line can exceed 30 minutes on summer afternoons (late May–early Sept) — arrive early or late in the day. Cashless entrance station — cards only.

Devils Tower has no formal International Dark Sky Association certification, but its remote location in rural northeastern Wyoming — far from major cities — means night skies are typically excellent. Best viewing spots are the Joyner Ridge Trail parking area and the Circle of Sacred Smoke Sculpture & picnic area, both recommended by NPS for minimal light pollution. Check Clear Dark Sky before you go, and bring a red flashlight to preserve night vision. NPS night sky info ↗
