⚠️Wildlife & Terrain SafetyBison, 30+ mph▾
🦬 Bison can run over 30 mph and cause fatal injuries — keep at least 100 feet away, always. Prairie rattlesnakes hide in shaded crevices and boardwalks; watch where you place hands and feet. The eroded badlands terrain causes most park injuries through falls, especially in the wrong footwear — wet clay buttes turn dangerously slick. Summer temperatures frequently top 100°F with little shade, and lightning can strike well ahead of a visible storm. Cell service is unavailable through most of the park, so carry a map and don't rely on your phone. NPS safety info ↗

🚗Badlands Loop RoadScenic drive▾
🗺️ SD-240 winds 31 miles through the park's most dramatic formations, with a string of overlooks (Pinnacles, Yellow Mounds, Panorama Point) and easy trailheads right off the pavement — the best way to see the park in a few hours. NPS scenic drive guide ↗
