🥾High-Altitude Hiking Safety13,063 ft summit▾
⛰️ Great Basin has one of the largest elevation ranges of any national park — the valley floor sits around 5,000 ft while Wheeler Peak tops out at 13,063 ft. The summit trail climbs well above treeline with no shelter from sun, wind, or lightning. Afternoon thunderstorms build fast in summer — start before sunrise and be off the exposed ridgeline by early afternoon. Watch for symptoms of altitude sickness (headache, nausea, dizziness) and turn back if they appear; there is no quick descent from the upper mountain. NPS safety info ↗

🪧Park Entrance & Cave ToursNo fee · ticketed caves▾
Main Access No Entrance Fee
NV-487, off US-50 near Baker, NV
Great Basin is one of the few national parks with no entrance fee at all. It's also one of the most remote — reached via US-50, "The Loneliest Road in America."
💡 There is no gas station inside the park. Fill up at Baker Sinclair in Baker, NV before heading in — it's the only fuel for miles.

Lehman Caves Ticketed Tours
Guided tours only, timed entry
The cave itself is only accessible on a ranger-led tour — no self-guided entry. Tickets sell out in summer; book ahead on Recreation.gov.
💡 Tour temperature stays around 50°F year-round — bring a light jacket even in summer.

Wheeler Peak Scenic Drive Seasonal
12 mi, roughly June – October
Climbs from the valley floor to nearly 10,000 ft near the Wheeler Peak trailhead. Closes with the first heavy snow and reopens once plowed in early summer.
💡 Check current road status before you go — closure timing varies year to year with snowpack.

🌙Stargazing & Night SkyGold tier▾

Loading…
Great Basin is an International Dark Sky Park, Gold Tier — the highest designation — and home to the Great Basin Observatory, the only research-grade observatory inside a US national park. The park hosts an annual Astronomy Festival each September plus regular ranger-led night-sky programs. Best on new-moon nights — bring a red flashlight to preserve night vision, and dress warm even in summer, as high-elevation nights get cold fast. NPS night sky info ↗
